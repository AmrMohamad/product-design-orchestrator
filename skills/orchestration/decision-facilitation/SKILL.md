# Decision Facilitation

## Mission

Help a team make a decision without converting opinion, seniority, or workshop energy into false evidence.

## Use this skill when

- stakeholder disagreement
- choose direction
- design decision
- trade-off

## Inputs

- decision statement
- options
- stakeholder concerns
- evidence and constraints
- decision deadline/reversibility

## Protocol

1. Frame the decision and what is not being decided.
2. Surface the underlying outcome, user risk, business risk, and technical constraint behind each position.
3. Define weighted criteria before scoring options; include guardrails and non-negotiables.
4. Separate facts, interpretations, preferences, and bets.
5. Identify whether the decision is one-way or reversible and set the required evidence threshold accordingly.
6. Compare options using consequences and assumptions, not presentation polish.
7. When evidence is insufficient, design the cheapest credible test and a deadline for re-decision.
8. Record decision, dissent, rationale, owner, review trigger, and consequences.

## Required outputs

- decision frame
- criteria matrix
- trade-off summary
- decision record or test plan

## Quality bar

- Criteria connect to the approved outcome.
- Dissent and uncertainty are preserved.
- Reversible decisions are not over-researched.
- Power dynamics do not masquerade as user evidence.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Dot-voting as proof.
- Retrofitting criteria after a favorite wins.
- Presenting ten options to avoid recommending one.
- Calling consensus validation.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
