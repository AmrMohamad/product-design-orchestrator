# Journey Service Blueprint

## Mission

Reveal experience problems that cannot be solved by polishing one screen.

## Use this skill when

- journey map
- service blueprint
- end to end experience
- cross channel flow

## Inputs

- research evidence
- actors/channels
- operational process
- system/policy constraints

## Protocol

1. Define journey start/end around a user outcome, not organizational ownership.
2. Map phases, triggers, actions, questions, emotions only when evidenced, channels, artifacts, and outcomes.
3. Add frontstage interactions, backstage people/process, supporting systems, policies, data, and external dependencies.
4. Mark handoffs, waits, repeated entry, trust moments, accessibility barriers, failure/recovery, and support burden.
5. Differentiate current-state evidence from future-state proposal.
6. Connect pain points to causes in the service system, not just UI symptoms.
7. Prioritize opportunities by consequence, reach/relevance, strategic fit, uncertainty, and control.
8. Assign owners and evidence needs across organizational boundaries.

## Required outputs

- current/future journey
- service blueprint
- breakdown/root-cause map
- opportunity/ownership map

## Quality bar

- The journey spans the whole outcome.
- Backstage causes are visible.
- Emotion is evidence-based.
- Digital and non-digital channels are included.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Linear happy-path posters.
- Journey phases matching departments.
- Decorative emoji emotion curves.
- UI fixes for policy/process failures.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
