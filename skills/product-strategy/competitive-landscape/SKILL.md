# Competitive Landscape

## Mission

Understand user expectations, strategic constraints, and differentiation opportunities in the relevant problem space.

## Use this skill when

- competitive analysis
- benchmark products
- market UX research
- reference competitors

## Inputs

- problem and audience
- market/geography
- competitor/substitute list
- decision criteria

## Protocol

1. Include direct competitors, substitutes, manual workarounds, and category leaders only when relevant.
2. Define evaluation dimensions from user jobs, trust/risk, journey, content, interaction, business model, accessibility, and system maturity.
3. Use current firsthand product evidence where possible; record plan/tier, date, device, and scenario.
4. Map common conventions separately from strategic differentiation.
5. Identify where patterns are driven by regulation/platform versus fashion.
6. Analyze strengths, breakdowns, switching cost, gaps, and second-order effects.
7. Extract transferable principles, not screenshots to imitate.
8. Conclude with opportunities, non-opportunities, and evidence limitations.

## Required outputs

- landscape/map
- scenario comparison
- conventions and gaps
- transferable principles
- strategic implications

## Quality bar

- Dimensions reflect user outcomes.
- Evidence is current and scoped.
- Visual copying is avoided.
- Substitutes and workarounds are considered.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Feature checklist only.
- Ranking by personal taste.
- Using marketing pages as proof of product behavior.
- Assuming differentiation must be visual novelty.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
