# Visual Design Skills

11 bundled skills.

## `anti-slop-taste`

Detect and remove generic AI-made visual patterns while preserving context, usability, brand, and deliberate familiar conventions.

## `color`

Build a perceptually coherent, accessible color system for brand, hierarchy, interaction, status, data, themes, and real display conditions.

## `creative-direction`

Translate audience, product, brand, and strategic intent into a coherent visual thesis, creative tension, system grammar, and signature move.

## `data-visualization`

Select, design, annotate, and implement charts and visual explanations that support accurate comparison, trend, distribution, relationship, and decision making.

## `design-lab`

Explore and compare meaningful design variants through focused questions, controlled changes, rendered options, and iterative feedback.

## `imagery-iconography`

Direct photography, illustration, diagrams, texture, icons, and media as a coherent semantic and brand system with accessible alternatives.

## `layout-composition`

Design responsive spatial systems, grids, hierarchy, rhythm, and optical balance from content and task rather than template geometry.

## `motion`

Design purposeful, interruptible, performant motion that explains continuity, causality, hierarchy, feedback, or spatial relationships and respects user preferences.

## `reference-analysis`

Analyze screenshots, products, brand systems, and visual references into transferable principles without copying their surface identity.

## `responsive-design`

Design adaptive experiences across viewport, device, orientation, input, content, and platform constraints rather than treating mobile as a scaled desktop.

## `typography`

Design and implement a readable, expressive, responsive type system with intentional hierarchy, rhythm, content fit, and language support.
