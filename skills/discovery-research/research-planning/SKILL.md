# Research Planning

## Mission

Make the study executable and capable of changing a product decision.

## Use this skill when

- research plan
- plan interviews
- study design
- research sprint

## Inputs

- decision context
- research questions
- timeline/budget
- target population
- existing evidence

## Protocol

1. Prioritize research questions by decision impact and uncertainty.
2. Define participant criteria by behavior/context; include accessibility and exclusion criteria carefully.
3. Select method and fidelity for each question; explain why alternatives are weaker.
4. Set sample strategy and stopping/learning criteria without promising statistical claims the design cannot support.
5. Plan recruitment, incentives, consent, privacy, accessibility accommodations, locations/tools, and observer roles.
6. Prepare discussion/task guide, neutral prompts, data capture, note structure, and incident plan.
7. Define session-level and cross-session analysis, contradiction handling, and team synthesis cadence.
8. Plan deliverables, decision meeting, ownership, and how findings enter the backlog/design artifacts.

## Required outputs

- research plan
- participant/recruitment brief
- method guide
- analysis plan
- ethics/privacy checklist
- decision schedule

## Quality bar

- Every question can influence a decision.
- Participant criteria match behavior.
- Analysis is planned before collection.
- The burden on participants is justified.

## Failure modes to avoid

- A list of interview questions called a plan.
- Convenience sampling without limitation.
- Collecting sensitive data by default.
- Research with no synthesis owner.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
