# Desk Research

## Mission

Answer a decision-relevant question with traceable, current, primary evidence and clearly bounded inference.

## Use this skill when

- web research
- competitive research
- standards research
- verify current information

## Inputs

- decision question
- geography/audience/date scope
- source constraints
- desired confidence

## Protocol

1. Decompose the question into claims that can change the decision.
2. Search primary/official sources first; use independent secondary sources for context or disagreement.
3. Record title, organization/author, publication/update date, access date, scope, and exact supporting section.
4. Compare event date with publication date and check version/current status for standards, laws, libraries, and roles.
5. Open the source and inspect surrounding context; do not rely on snippets.
6. Seek counterevidence and explain credible disagreement.
7. Separate source claims from synthesis and inference.
8. Rate relevance and confidence; identify missing evidence and time-sensitive assumptions.
9. Produce a decision-focused synthesis rather than a link dump.

## Required outputs

- source ledger
- claim-evidence table
- contradictions
- synthesis and recommendation
- freshness risks

## Quality bar

- Load-bearing claims trace to sources.
- Current facts are verified.
- Sources are diverse and authoritative for the topic.
- Quotes are minimal and accurately represented.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Search snippets as evidence.
- SEO listicles for technical truth.
- Cherry-picking.
- Invented citations.
- Research without a decision.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
