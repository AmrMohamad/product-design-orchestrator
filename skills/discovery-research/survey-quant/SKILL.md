# Survey Quant

## Mission

Estimate patterns or compare groups without using numbers to give weak questions a false appearance of rigor.

## Use this skill when

- survey
- quantitative UX research
- measure prevalence
- questionnaire

## Inputs

- decision/construct
- target population
- sampling access
- existing qualitative evidence
- analysis capability

## Protocol

1. Define the construct and decision; decide whether a survey is the appropriate method.
2. Translate each construct into one clear question or validated scale where available.
3. Use mutually exclusive/exhaustive choices when appropriate and include "not applicable/prefer not to say" deliberately.
4. Avoid leading, loaded, double-barreled, recall-heavy, and absolute questions.
5. Pilot for comprehension, response burden, device behavior, and data quality.
6. Document sampling frame, recruitment, response rate, weighting, exclusions, missing data, and limitations.
7. Predefine primary analyses and segmentation; avoid fishing for impressive correlations.
8. Report uncertainty and practical effect, not just percentages or significance.
9. Pair quantitative patterns with qualitative/context evidence when explaining why.

## Required outputs

- instrument
- sampling/recruitment plan
- analysis plan
- data-quality rules
- result/limitations report

## Quality bar

- Questions measure the intended construct.
- Sampling supports the claimed population.
- Privacy/minimization are respected.
- Charts expose denominators and uncertainty.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Using a survey for discovery of unknown mental models.
- Reporting convenience sample as market truth.
- Leading scales.
- Data dredging.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
