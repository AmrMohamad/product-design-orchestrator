# Discovery Research Skills

10 bundled skills.

## `contextual-inquiry`

Observe people performing real work in context to uncover tacit behavior, environmental constraints, artifacts, interruptions, and workarounds.

## `desk-research`

Conduct current, source-grounded desk research for product, market, policy, standards, patterns, and technology decisions.

## `inclusive-research`

Plan and conduct research that includes disabled people and participants facing language, literacy, device, bandwidth, confidence, or access barriers.

## `product-discovery`

Understand the problem space, users, current journey, constraints, and opportunity before committing to a production solution.

## `research-planning`

Create a proportionate research plan that connects decisions and assumptions to participants, methods, logistics, analysis, and ethics.

## `research-synthesis`

Turn real qualitative and quantitative evidence into themes, models, opportunities, and decisions while preserving contradictions and traceability.

## `robust-research`

Design and govern credible mixed-method product research with explicit evidence labels, bias controls, contradictions, ethics, and confidence.

## `survey-quant`

Design surveys and quantitative studies that measure the right construct with honest sampling, analysis, and uncertainty.

## `usability-testing`

Evaluate whether representative people can complete realistic tasks, understand system feedback, and recover from problems in an appropriate-fidelity prototype or product.

## `user-interviews`

Plan, moderate, and analyze user interviews that reveal context, behavior, motivations, constraints, and mental models without pitching the solution.
