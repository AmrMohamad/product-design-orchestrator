# Research Synthesis

## Mission

Create insight that is more than a summary and more honest than a dramatic story.

## Use this skill when

- synthesize research
- affinity mapping
- analyze interviews
- research findings

## Inputs

- session notes/transcripts
- analytics/operational data
- research questions
- participant/context metadata

## Protocol

1. Clean and label evidence; keep participant/source and context traceable.
2. Analyze sessions individually before cross-session clustering.
3. Cluster bottom-up around behavior, cause, need, barrier, strategy, and consequence—not prewritten feature buckets.
4. Actively search for negative cases, contradictions, and segment/context differences.
5. Separate observation, pattern, interpretation, insight, opportunity, and recommendation.
6. Connect each finding to evidence coverage and confidence; avoid quote counting as prevalence.
7. Build the model best suited to the decision: journey, JTBD, mental model, taxonomy, opportunity map, or task breakdown.
8. Prioritize implications by user consequence, strategic relevance, uncertainty, and feasibility.
9. Review synthesis with the team and revise when interpretation outruns evidence.

## Required outputs

- evidence clusters
- findings with traceability/confidence
- contradictions/segments
- model/opportunity map
- decision implications

## Quality bar

- Findings explain why they matter.
- Raw evidence remains retrievable.
- Contradictions are visible.
- Recommendations do not exceed confidence.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Theme labels like "simplicity" with no behavior.
- Cherry-picked quotes.
- Personas invented from assumptions.
- Losing provenance during affinity mapping.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
