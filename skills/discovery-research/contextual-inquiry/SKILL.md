# Contextual Inquiry

## Mission

See the parts of a service or workflow that people cannot easily recall or describe in an interview.

## Use this skill when

- field research
- contextual inquiry
- observe workflow
- shadow users

## Inputs

- target activity/context
- access and consent
- safety/privacy limits
- observation questions

## Protocol

1. Define the activity and moments to observe, including before/after the digital touchpoint.
2. Secure organizational and participant consent; define recording and sensitive-data boundaries.
3. Adopt apprentice posture: let the participant lead and explain at natural pauses.
4. Record environment, actors, tools/artifacts, sequence, handoffs, interruptions, cues, workarounds, and failure recovery.
5. Ask short clarifying questions without turning observation into a retrospective interview.
6. Distinguish standard process from local adaptation and exceptional behavior.
7. Debrief with workflow map, evidence, uncertainty, and operational/system implications.
8. Validate sensitive interpretations with participants or domain experts when appropriate.

## Required outputs

- observation guide
- workflow/context map
- artifact inventory
- breakdown/workaround findings
- design implications

## Quality bar

- Observation includes non-digital context.
- Privacy and workplace power are considered.
- Tacit behavior is captured without judgment.
- Findings do not generalize beyond evidence.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Observing only the happy path.
- Treating policy as actual behavior.
- Recording sensitive screens casually.
- Interrupting every action with questions.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
