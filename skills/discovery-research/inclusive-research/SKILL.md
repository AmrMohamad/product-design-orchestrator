# Inclusive Research

## Mission

Prevent the easiest sample and default environment from defining the product for everyone.

## Use this skill when

- inclusive research
- research disabled users
- accessibility research
- hard to reach users

## Inputs

- target journey
- known exclusion risks
- participant communities
- research logistics/tools

## Protocol

1. Map who may be excluded by the service, recruitment channel, method, location, technology, language, or compensation model.
2. Recruit by relevant access need and context without reducing people to a diagnosis.
3. Ask participants about accommodations, communication preference, support person, assistive technology, and session pacing.
4. Test the research tool/prototype itself for accessibility before the session.
5. Allow alternative participation modes and accessible consent/materials.
6. Observe the participant with their own setup where possible; do not take over their task.
7. Separate barriers caused by the prototype, service, research environment, and external system.
8. Compensate fairly and protect sensitive disability/access information.
9. Translate findings into systemic requirements rather than isolated exceptions.

## Required outputs

- exclusion map
- inclusive recruitment/accommodation plan
- accessible session materials
- barrier findings
- system requirements

## Quality bar

- Participants can choose how to engage.
- The study covers the whole task.
- Assistive technology findings are contextual.
- Accessibility is not delegated to one participant.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Recruiting one disabled person as certification.
- Inaccessible prototypes.
- Medicalizing product barriers.
- Assuming a screen reader represents all disability.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
