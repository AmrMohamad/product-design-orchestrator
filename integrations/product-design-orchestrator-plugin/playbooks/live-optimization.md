# Playbook — Improve a live product

1. Verify instrumentation health and define the user outcome, segments, baseline, guardrails, and operational context.
2. Combine field behavior/performance, accessibility, support/ops, research, reliability, and trust/safety signals.
3. Diagnose the journey and causal model before changing the interface. Do not optimize a proxy while harming task success.
4. Rank opportunities by evidence, outcome leverage, harm, reversibility, and cost.
5. Use research, staged rollout, experiment, or direct fix according to uncertainty and ethics; not every defect needs an A/B test.
6. Implement through the system, with tests, flags/rollback, and observability.
7. Evaluate adequate windows and segments; preserve inconclusive/negative results.
8. Update components, content, decision records, and guidance so learning compounds.
9. Monitor regressions and second-order harms.
10. Retire low-value or harmful behavior with migration and communication.
