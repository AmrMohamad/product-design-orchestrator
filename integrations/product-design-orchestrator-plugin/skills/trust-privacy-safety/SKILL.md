# Trust Privacy Safety

## Mission

Make the product worthy of trust rather than merely looking trustworthy.

## Use this skill when

- trust
- privacy UX
- safety critical UI
- consent
- dangerous action

## Inputs

- data flows and permissions
- risk/threat model
- user journey
- legal/policy constraints
- support/escalation paths

## Protocol

1. Map affected people, power imbalance, assets/data, threats, misuse, accidental harm, and irreversible consequences.
2. Minimize collection, exposure, retention, and default sharing; make purpose and control legible.
3. Design consent as informed and specific, not a barrier designed to be dismissed.
4. Use progressive friction for high-risk actions: preview, confirmation, authentication, delay, approval, undo/recovery as appropriate.
5. Expose automation/AI, confidence/limitations, provenance, and human escalation when material.
6. Protect against coercive hierarchy, hidden cost, confirmshaming, forced continuity, obstruction, and urgency theater.
7. Design incident/error states that preserve evidence, dignity, and safe next actions.
8. Review with legal/security/domain experts without delegating product clarity to policy text.

## Required outputs

- risk/agency map
- data/consent requirements
- high-risk interaction contract
- misuse/incident states
- expert-review checklist

## Quality bar

- User control is real and reversible where possible.
- Material consequences are clear before commitment.
- Data minimization is reflected in UI and architecture.
- Trust is backed by behavior.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Trust badges as a substitute for security.
- Prechecked consent.
- Dark patterns.
- Hiding AI or uncertainty.
- Confirm dialogs for every low-risk action.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
