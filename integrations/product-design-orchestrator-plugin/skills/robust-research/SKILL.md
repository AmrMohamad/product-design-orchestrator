# Robust Research

## Mission

Make research strong enough for the consequence of the decision while preventing AI-generated plausibility from becoming fake evidence.

## Use this skill when

- research rigor
- robust research
- evidence quality
- AI research

## Inputs

- decision and risk
- known evidence
- participants/data sources
- time/privacy/access constraints

## Protocol

1. State the decision, research question, current belief, and evidence that would change the belief.
2. Choose methods by question: generative versus evaluative, qualitative versus quantitative, attitudinal versus behavioral, context versus controlled task.
3. Define the relevant population, behavioral criteria, exclusions, hard-to-reach segments, and sampling limitations.
4. Create a method protocol, consent/data handling plan, moderator/measurement plan, and analysis approach before collection.
5. Label every datum OBSERVED, REPORTED, MEASURED, INFERRED, ASSUMED, or SYNTHETIC.
6. Capture negative cases, contradictions, uncertainty, and researcher/model influence.
7. Triangulate high-impact findings with an appropriate second method or operational signal when feasible.
8. Separate frequency within the sample from population prevalence.
9. Assess confidence based on relevance, quality, convergence, and limitations—not number of quotes or links.
10. Translate findings into decisions, rejected interpretations, and next evidence triggers.

## Required outputs

- research protocol
- evidence ledger
- bias/ethics controls
- confidence assessment
- decision implications

## Quality bar

- No synthetic participant is described as real.
- Methods fit questions and consequences.
- Contradictions remain visible.
- Findings distinguish observation from interpretation.
- Privacy and informed consent are explicit.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Five-user mythology as a universal sample rule.
- Counting comments as prevalence.
- Leading questions.
- Using model consensus as triangulation.
- Erasing inconvenient evidence.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
