# User Interviews

## Mission

Learn how people currently experience the problem and make decisions in context.

## Use this skill when

- interview users
- discussion guide
- generative interview
- customer interview

## Inputs

- research question
- participant criteria
- known assumptions
- consent/privacy plan

## Protocol

1. Create a funnel from context and recent concrete events to behavior, artifacts, workarounds, consequences, and reflection.
2. Use neutral, open prompts and ask for specific past examples before opinions or hypotheticals.
3. Avoid describing the solution until the problem portion is complete.
4. Probe sequence, environment, actors, tools, triggers, exceptions, emotional/social cost, and decision criteria.
5. Ask permission for sensitive topics and allow skipping/ending without penalty.
6. Capture verbatim evidence separately from moderator interpretation.
7. Debrief immediately: observations, surprises, contradictions, hypotheses, and follow-up changes.
8. Synthesize across participants; do not over-index on eloquence or one memorable quote.

## Required outputs

- discussion guide
- consent/opening script
- structured notes
- session debrief
- cross-session themes/limits

## Quality bar

- Questions do not imply the desired answer.
- Recent behavior precedes preference.
- Quotes retain context.
- Participant dignity and privacy are protected.

## Failure modes to avoid

- "Would you use this?" as value proof.
- Selling during research.
- Asking compound questions.
- Treating reported intent as behavior.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
