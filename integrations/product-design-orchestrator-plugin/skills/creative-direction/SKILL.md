# Creative Direction

## Mission

Create a direction that belongs to the product rather than applying a generic aesthetic preset.

## Use this skill when

- visual direction
- art direction
- design style
- make it distinctive

## Inputs

- brief/outcomes
- audience/context
- brand assets
- references/competitors
- platform/content

## Protocol

1. Write a Design Read and identify the emotional/functional job of the visual language.
2. Define three precise qualities and their opposites; add one productive creative tension.
3. Generate at least three structurally different direction theses for consequential work.
4. For each direction define type, composition, density, color role, material/image behavior, motion grammar, and one signature move.
5. Calibrate variance, motion, density, warmth, materiality, and convention.
6. Apply the direction to realistic content and at least one difficult state, not a hero-only mood.
7. Evaluate against user/task fit, brand truth, accessibility, implementation, maintainability, and differentiation.
8. Converge with rationale and record what is deliberately excluded.

## Required outputs

- direction theses
- visual grammar
- dial settings
- signature move
- selected direction/rationale

## Quality bar

- Direction is specific enough to guide multiple screens.
- Character comes from selection and composition, not effects.
- Real content/states are shown.
- Aesthetic supports task and audience.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Moodboard without decisions.
- Style keywords only.
- Copying one brand.
- Making every element distinctive.
- Confusing luxury with low contrast.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
