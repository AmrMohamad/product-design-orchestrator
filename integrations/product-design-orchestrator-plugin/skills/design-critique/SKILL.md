# Design Critique

## Mission

Make quality visible and improvable without reducing critique to subjective taste or a generic checklist.

## Use this skill when

- critique UI
- design review
- audit screen
- why does this feel off

## Inputs

- brief/outcome
- rendered screens at relevant states/sizes
- flows and component/system context
- known evidence

## Protocol

1. Restate the intended user outcome and visual/behavioral thesis.
2. Inspect the complete task and real rendered states, not a single ideal frame.
3. Identify what works and must be preserved before proposing change.
4. Review in order: task/trust/accessibility, structure/content, interaction/state, system consistency, visual craft, delight.
5. Classify findings S0–S4 and connect each to observed consequence.
6. For every material issue, propose a correction and why it should improve the outcome.
7. Separate defects, opportunities, and taste experiments.
8. Recommend the smallest high-leverage sequence and specify how to verify it.

## Required outputs

- intent summary
- preserve list
- prioritized findings
- correction sequence
- verification plan

## Quality bar

- Critique is based on rendered evidence.
- Severity reflects user/product consequence.
- Corrections are concrete.
- The critique does not redesign everything by reflex.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Aesthetic adjectives without evidence.
- Starting with micro-spacing while the flow is wrong.
- Personal attacks or performative harshness.
- Calling novelty quality.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
