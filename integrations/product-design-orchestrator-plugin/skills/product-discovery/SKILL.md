# Product Discovery

## Mission

Establish whether there is a problem worth solving and what evidence is needed to proceed, reframe, or stop.

## Use this skill when

- discovery phase
- understand users
- new product problem
- before solution

## Inputs

- initial request
- stakeholder context
- existing research/data
- service/product landscape

## Protocol

1. Reframe the requested feature as a problem hypothesis without erasing the request.
2. Identify primary users, excluded/non-users, support and operational actors, and decision makers.
3. Map current behavior, workarounds, adjacent channels, and cost of the problem.
4. Inspect prior research, analytics, support logs, policies, market context, and comparable services.
5. List hard constraints, soft constraints, dependencies, risks, and unknowns.
6. Create research questions tied to proceed/reframe/stop decisions.
7. Map assumptions by uncertainty and consequence; prioritize the riskiest.
8. Define discovery exit evidence and what would make the team not build.

## Required outputs

- discovery frame
- actor/current-journey map
- evidence/unknown inventory
- assumption map
- proceed/reframe/stop criteria

## Quality bar

- The need is not phrased as the proposed feature.
- The whole journey and operations are considered.
- Discovery includes accessibility and exclusion risks.
- No production build is used as a substitute for understanding.

## Failure modes to avoid

- Solution interviews.
- Research theater with no decision.
- Only speaking to internal stakeholders.
- Treating competitor features as user needs.

## Handoff

Record consequential decisions and pass only decision-relevant context to the next phase.
