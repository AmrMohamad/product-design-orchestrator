# Playbook — Greenfield product

Use when product, behavior, and system are materially unknown. Do not confuse a visual prototype with proof of a viable service.

1. **Orient** — define sponsor, intended audience, operating domain, consequence of error, constraints, technology reality, and initial Design Read.
2. **Discover** — triangulate stakeholder/service data, existing behavior/substitutes, field/user research, domain/market evidence, and inclusion/safety concerns. Maintain evidence and assumption ledgers.
3. **Define** — choose one problem/outcome, scope the whole journey, establish measures and guardrails, and reject premature feature commitments.
4. **Explore** — create meaningfully different service/interaction/visual models. Test the riskiest assumptions with the lowest sufficient fidelity.
5. **Structure** — model objects, IA, flows, permissions, content, operational handoffs, and failure/recovery before detailed styling.
6. **Systemize/prototype** — establish a coherent foundation, tokens, components, states, and representative end-to-end prototype.
7. **Validate** — use real target participants and realistic tasks; preserve contradictions; decide act/test/hold.
8. **Implement** — inspect stack, plan vertical slices, build real states and instrumentation, update contracts when reality changes.
9. **Verify/release** — browser/device/accessibility/visual/performance/resilience/privacy/operations gates, controlled rollout and rollback.
10. **Live** — measure outcomes and harms, continue research, update system/docs, retire features responsibly.

Exit only when the decision is evidence-backed, the rendered experience is verified, and live ownership exists.
