# Playbook — High-consequence, regulated, identity, financial, health, or vulnerable-user service

1. Bring domain, legal/compliance, privacy/security, accessibility, content, operations, support, and representative users into the decision early.
2. Map harms, misuse/abuse, error consequences, power asymmetry, coercion, consent, data lifecycle, and recovery/escalation.
3. Research with appropriate ethics, safeguarding, privacy, accessibility, and expert oversight. Never substitute AI personas for lived experience.
4. Design comprehension, agency, confirmation, reversibility, status, evidence, human help, and accountable handoffs.
5. Minimize data and dark patterns; make consequences and alternatives legible.
6. Validate with realistic high-risk scenarios, assistive technology and stress/error conditions as warranted.
7. Implement defense in depth: safe defaults, robust permissions, auditability, failure containment, observability, and operational ownership.
8. Require formal accessibility/privacy/security/domain reviews and resolve S0/S1 findings before release.
9. Stage rollout with rollback, support, incident response, and harm/quality guardrails.
10. Continuously monitor, research, report, and improve; compliance is a floor, not proof of usability or safety.
