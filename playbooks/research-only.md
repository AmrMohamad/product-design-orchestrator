# Playbook — Robust product/UX research

1. Name the decision, uncertainty, consequence, and existing evidence before selecting a method.
2. Separate generative/evaluative, qualitative/quantitative, attitudinal/behavioral needs.
3. Recruit for relevant behavior/context; include people commonly excluded; plan accommodations, consent, privacy, safeguarding, and compensation.
4. Use a sample and method proportionate to heterogeneity and decision risk; never use a ritual number as proof.
5. Pilot realistic tasks/questions/stimuli; control leading language and moderator bias.
6. Capture evidence traceably. Do not treat stakeholder opinion, AI synthesis, or synthetic users as participants.
7. Analyze systematically; preserve contradiction, negative cases, context, uncertainty, and study limitations.
8. Triangulate when sources can answer different parts of the question.
9. Translate findings into decision consequences and act/test/hold recommendations, not a decorative report.
10. Store evidence safely, communicate back to the team, and define what would change the conclusion.
